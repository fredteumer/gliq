# Mimic

## Concept
A Diablo-like Auto-looter where a player equips a character before each "run" and the character auto-ARPGs through the level. Player mashes clicks to pick things up for action.

## Core mechanics
After equipping the character for the run (potions, gear from equip bank), character plays on an isometric grid level and runs around auto-killing mobs. Run difficulty increases run-run and offers roguelite mechanics.

Characters are unlocked after N runs or completing secondary objectives randomly decided on each run.

3 main classes at launch, more later as DLC.

Character returns from run with loot (or not if they die) and you choose what to equip/bank/sell/buy from the vendor in the "waiting room".

Player interaction is watching the madness, clicking the loot drops during the runs, and then decisions on items/equips/ability selection for the characters. 

Some gold + items are preserved run-run for light roguelite mechanics.

## Art & audio
Hand-drawn frank-frazetta aesthetics of low fantasy. Gritty, but not dark. Dark Sun meets Bastion. 

Sound design is brutal and crunchy. Big, meaty hits on fleshy targets, clang of metal, jingle of coin. 

## Platforms & pricing

Windows only. Priced at $5.99 with additional DLC for character packs.
